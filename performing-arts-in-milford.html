Frequently Asked Questions About Performing Arts

What is the cost for enrolling my child in the performing arts program at Camp Netimus, and are there any additional fees for materials or costumes?
The performing arts program is included in Camp Netimus’s overall tuition, which varies depending on the session length you choose. For the 2024 season in Milford, PA, full session tuition is $8,800, with shorter sessions available at a prorated rate. While most materials are provided, there may be a small, one-time fee (usually under $50) if your child participates in a major production requiring special costumes or props. All fees will be communicated in advance, and there are no surprise charges.
When during the summer do performing arts activities take place, and how are rehearsals and shows scheduled around other camp activities?
Performing arts workshops, rehearsals, and classes are offered daily throughout all camp sessions, typically in the afternoons or early evenings. Camp Netimus coordinates performing arts schedules so campers can participate fully without missing out on other activities like swimming or hiking. Major performances are usually held near the end of each session, allowing families to attend if possible. Detailed schedules are provided before camp begins and updated weekly.
Is Camp Netimus licensed or accredited for its performing arts programs, and what qualifications do your instructors have?
Camp Netimus is fully licensed by the Pennsylvania Department of Health and is accredited by the American Camp Association, ensuring high standards in both safety and programming. Our performing arts staff are experienced professionals, many with degrees in theater, dance, or music education, and several return each year to lead specialized workshops. All instructors undergo background checks and extensive pre-camp training.
How does my child sign up for specific performing arts activities, and can they participate in more than one performance during their stay?
After registering for Camp Netimus, you’ll receive an activity preference form where your child can indicate their interests, including specific performing arts options like drama, dance, music, or stagecraft. Campers are encouraged to explore multiple areas and may audition for more than one performance or join several workshops. Our staff helps each camper create a balanced schedule so they can pursue their passions while enjoying the full camp experience.
What happens if my child is unable to perform due to illness or needs to leave camp early—are there any guarantees or refund policies in place?
While we can’t guarantee every camper will perform due to unforeseen circumstances, our staff works closely with families to provide alternative roles or activities if a camper misses a show. For early departures or illness, Camp Netimus offers prorated refunds in certain cases, following our published refund policy. Please contact our Milford, PA office as soon as possible if your child’s attendance changes, and we’ll do our best to accommodate your family’s needs.
